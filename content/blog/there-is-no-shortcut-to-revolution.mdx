I have been onboarding “normies” lately into Ethereum in Vietnam. Again, despite the popular claim that Vietnam is leading in crypto adoption, situation on the ground is different. 

There are non stop unethical attempts by questionable centralized exchanges (like MEXC, BingX, ATX, Onus, etc.) and KOLs luring uninformed individuals into degenerate gambling and unsustainble high yield schemes, then freeze their accounts, steal their funds, or unload dogshit coins. I do my best to call them out (my facebook reach is pretty solid) but they pay big for marketing spotlights and recruit high profile folks to “clean up” their reputation.

On the other hand, me, a solo builder with practically zero budget, on a quest to truly onboard people into Ethereum via my own conviction. I have staked years of my life already and will continue to do so even when it comes at great personal cost. I was determined to not follow any of the popular methods to onboard “normies” like making it super easy, or download an exchange app, or do all the technical mental gymnastic. I didn’t believe in those methods anyways. We have been parroting the same delusion about the billions everyday person that would come into crypto to use our tech and buy our bags. As of today, are we on track to at least make that delusion into reality or is it a mirage that we can never touch?

For those who don’t know, Vietnam went through a period of economic mismangement so bad that even the government had to admit publicly (they couldn’t even sweep it under the rug). Everyone’s parents (50 yrs and above) would have a story about their experience during this time. Not just that, a currency conversion practically wiped out many people savings in banks, forcing people to use gold as a medium of exchange (it was during US sanction so very limited access to USD), rampant inflation and rock bottom poverty. My approach is based on this historical fact, that’s based on publicly available information and not some made up story to smear the government of Vietnam. To be fair, they have done good things to take Vietnam from a war torn country to what it is today.

So my approach is focusing on “self sovereign”, with Ethereum being the safe haven alternative financial system. I don’t tell people that the Vietnam payment system sucks. It’s actually quite good, because I actually worked with it not too long ago. I have done architecture design and integration work with NAPAS, the local debit network, for both interbank and payment card rails. Local banks also make it free for customers to use it despite them paying fees for every transaction to NAPAS. They have a shared QR standard (super simple as well) among all the banks and fintech. Transactions are finalized (locked in for real not just authorized) within 1-2 seconds. Accounts are mostly free and the unbank problem is getting close to being solved. I don’t tell people about any “highly profitable opportunities” on Ethereum waiting for them to pile their money in. I also take into account of Vietnam national interest with its weaker currency against the US Dollar so I don’t advocate buying USDT. I instead focus on helping people to understand this idea of “self sovereign” of their assets with ETH being the hardest asset of all.
